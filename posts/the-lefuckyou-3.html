The LeFuckYou 3

Does LeBron ever miss them?

A term coined by the all too entertaining NBA subreddit, the LeFuckYou 3 is my second favorite shot in the NBA (first spot goes out to Dirk Nowitzki's silky smooth one-legged fadeaway). For those unfamiliar with the LeFuckYou 3, just take a look at the clip below.

Though Steph Curry was arguably the pioneer in expanding NBA 3-point ranges, LeBron's stone cold demeaner while launching these long bombs won him the naming rights to this shot. Oh, and also the fact that he seems like guaranteed money from that range. But is he actually as accurate as he seems?

For the purposes of this exercise, a LeFuckYou 3 has been defined as a three-pointer taken from 30-34 feet. Shots further than this were excluded due to the majority of them being buzzer beaters taken at the end of a quarter. Out of the 479 players who played in the 2017-2018 regular season, only 22 of them attempted more than 5 three's from LeFuckYou range.

The sample size is obviously tiny. Let's get that out of the way. With that being said, LeBron hits LeFuckYou's at an unreal 63.6% clip. To put that number into perspective, consider that LeBron's 3-point percentage was 36.7%, his FG percentage was 54.2%, and his free throw percentage was 73.1%. He's also 18 percentage points better than Spencer Dinwiddie, the surprising number 2 in LeFuckYou 3-point %. Besides LeBron, the other standout (in a much less positive way) is Damian Lillard. Dame took 36 LeFuckYou 3's this season, 14 more than Dinwiddie, who had the next highest number of attempts.

So basically, LeBron doesn't miss LeFuckYou 3's. And someone please tell Damian Lillard that he's not Steph Curry.
