Predicting Roger Federer's Wikipedia Activity with Prophet

Using Facebook's Prophet to Forecast Page Views

A couple of weeks ago, one of my coworker's introduced me to Prophet, and since then I've been meaning to mess around with the software to create some forecasts. At my previous job, an enormous amount of effort went into creating time series regression models for forecasts, and the idea of a tool that makes forecasting so much quicker and accessible was really intriguing. I finally got around to choosing a data set I could work with, which ended up being the daily number of page views for the wikipedia page of the greatest tennis player of all time.

Data for this project was obtained from ToolForge, and is plotted out below. The chart on the left shows daily page views going back until July 2015. The data appears to have lulls with low volume, with sudden and large order of magnitude spikes during short periods. This checks out, as most tennis fans really only tune in during Grand Slams. To verify this, I've investigated some of the spikes in the data:

  • January 29, 2017 - Roger wins the Australian Open
  • July 16, 2017 - Roger wins Wimbledon
  • January 28, 2018 - Roger wins the Australian Open

Due to the multiplicative nature of the data, I logged all the data to make it easier to model, which is shown on the chart to the right. I also split the data into a training and testing set for crass validation, with the last year of data being held out for the test set. Plugging the training data into Prophet provided the following forecast and plot components.

The initial forecast seems to do a decent job at catching the periods of lower volume and inflection points, but misses by a significant order of magnitude on the spikes. Regarding the trend components, we can see that the trend goes up in 2017 (coinciding with Federer's comeback season when he won two majors), and has been tailing off since. Sunday and Monday are the days that have the highest volume, and the yearly seasonality follows the ATP tour's schedule very closely, seeing higher influences during grand slam tournaments and the ATP Tour Masters 1000 tournaments. To measure the forecast's accuracy, I calculated the MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) for the logged forecast, which was 42.4%.

With the basic forecast out of the way, I sought to minimize the MAPE through a variety of methods (adding holidays, changing the growth parameter, modifying prior scales), but was only able to find success with adding the dates of the grand slams to the model.

With the new information provided to the model, the MAPE comes down to 33.6%, and the large spikes fit more nicely. However, there are still a number of spikes that the model fails to capture. Listed are a few of the highest discrepancies in the forecast period:

  • January 5, 2019 - Roger wins Hopman Cup
  • March 2, 2019 - Roger wins Dubai Tennis Championships
  • March 31, 2019 - Roger wins Miami Open

These are all events that Federer won in 2019, but did not have as much success during the dates our forecast was modeled off of. The page views are highly influenced by tournament wins, and not having successful results in our modeling period hurts our forecast. While forecasting Roger Federer's page views might be a difficult task due to the heavy influence of championships, having a longer history of data, and including date ranges of the tournaments Federer frequently plays in should help improve the accuracy of the forecast.
