Beating the Golden State Warriors

An evolving post that will attempt to shed light on any and all weaknesses the Warriors may have.

After winning their third championship in four years, the Golden State Warriors retained their core, while also adding 4x NBA All-Star, DeMarcus Cousins. It seems as unfair as it ever has for the rest of the league, as they'll have to go up against 5 All-Star caliber players. Throughout the 2018-2019 NBA Season, I'm going to attempt to identify weaknesses that other teams in the NBA can exploit against the Warriors.

Shot Charts

A huge part of the Warrior's success comes from their ability to space the floor. Their 3-point shooters draw defenders out, in effect expanding the court. While defenders can't magically get faster or longer, they can defend smarter. By taking a look at where the Warriors like to take shots and how successful they are at each spot in the court, opponents can focus on covering areas where the Warriors excel, and give up space where the Warriors are less efficient. First, let's take a look at a shot chart of the entire GSW team. The cold/hot and less/more numbers are relative to league averages.

Looking at this chart, the Warriors are significantly less efficient at three areas of the court: both sidelines & outside the mid-range to the right of the basket.

Once we identify the team's strengths, we can go a step further and look at the same view for each individual on the team, so we can inform defenders with this more granular information.
