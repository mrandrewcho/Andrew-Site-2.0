Should You be Excited for the 2018 NBA Draft?

Evaluating the historical drafting performances of NBA franchises.

I've come to hate the NBA draft. As a lifelong Pistons fan (2010-2015 was pretty tough to watch), the draft seems to lead only to disappointment. Most recently, we passed up on Donovan Mitchell for Luke Kennard, took Stanley Johnson while Devin Booker was still on the board, selected Brandon Knight over Kemba Walker, and of course infamously drafted Darko Milicic over Carmelo Anthony, Chris Bosh, AND Dwyane Wade. The 2018 draft, coming up on June 21, won't change much either, as the Pistons traded away their first round pick as part of the Blake Griffin deal.

While the Pistons haven't had much draft success in my lifetime, they've at least had some quality picks such as Isiah Thomas and Joe Dumars. This led me to question: which franchise is the worst at drafting?

To quantify a franchises drafting prowess, I calculated a metric called "Draft Score" for each pick each franchise made. Draft Score = Drafted Player's Lifetime Win Shares - Average Win Share for that Pick Number across all drafts. Due to Lifetime Win Shares being used, I removed the last 5 drafts, as these players have not had enough time to provide positive value. The drafts evaluated are 1989 (first year 2 rounds were used) to 2013. Below are the franchises ranked, with their cumulative draft scores over the years.
