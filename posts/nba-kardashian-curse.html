The NBA Kardashian Curse?

Exploring how the Kardashian family has impacted the performances of their NBA partners

A while back, I was listening to the Bill Simmons Podcast (around the 95 minute mark), where Bill and recurring guest Ryen Russillo were discussing why Ben Simmons didn't make the leap forward that he was expected to make in his sophomore season in the NBA. Bill suggests that Ben's relationship with Kendall Jenner may have been a contributing factor, to which Ryen poses that it would be interesting to see stats on the effects that celebrity girlfriends/wives have had on the performance of NBA athletes.

Unfortunately, I wasn't able to identify a good source of data for NBA celebrity relationships, so I had to resort to searching for tabloid articles and cross referencing them with other articles to establish start and end dates for relationships. In additon, there wasn't reliable data for lesser known celebrities, so to make things as simple as possible, I chose to focus on the Kardashian family. After a healthy number of Google searches, the list of relationships I compiled are the following:

  • Blake Griffin & Kendall Jenner (Relationship Period: August 2017 - February 2018)
  • Ben Simmons & Kendall Jenner (Relationship Period: June 12, 2018 - May 22, 2019)
  • Lamar Odom & Khloe Kardashian (Relationship Period: August 2009 - December 13, 2013)
  • Tristan Thompson & Khloe Kardashian (Relationship Period: July 2016 - February 19, 2019)
  • Kris Humphries & Kim Kardashian (Relationship Period: November 2010 - November 2011)

With the relationships taken care of, the last thing I had to do before starting analysis was decide on a performance metric to look at. I used Game Scores, as they were readily available at basketball-reference.com. The formula is below:

Game Score = (PTS + 0.4) * (FG - 0.7) * (FGA - 0.4)*((FTA - FT) + 0.7 )* (ORB + 0.3) * DRB + (STL + 0.7) * (AST + 0.7 )* (BLK - 0.4 )* PF - TOV

I also transformed the Game Scores into a rolling 2-week average to account for the high level of variation from game to game.

Blake Griffin only plays 34 games during his relationship with Kendall Jenner, and his average game score is identical during and outside of his relationship at 17.

Ben Simmons played his entire second season while in a relationship with Kendall Jenner, and his average game scores are very similar at 16.6 during the relationship and 15.8 outside of the relationship.

Lamar Odom plays a good chunk of his career while in a relationship with Khloe Kardashian, and performs poorly while dating Khloe. During the relationship, his average game score is 8.3, while outside of it his average game score is 12.4. A two-sided t-test reveals that the mean game scores are statistically different. A key caveat is that Lamar dates Khloe at the end of his career, so there is the possibility that the decline in productivity was simply due to his age.

Despite dating Khloe Kardashian for nearly 3 years, Tristan only records 88 games played during that period. In those games, his average game score is 8.3, and outside of those games his average game score is 8.7.

Khris Humphries plays 71 games while dating Kim Kardashian, and surprisingly has a much higher average game score during that period. During his relationship, his average game score is 10.1, and outside of that his average game score is 5.1. A two-sided t-test shows that these means are statistically different.

Out of the 5 samples I looked at, 3 players performed more or less in line with their career averages while dating a Kardashian, 1 player (Lamar Odom) performed worse, and 1 player (Khris Humphries) performed better. There isn't too much to take away from such a small sample of games and relationships, but it is interesting that a lot of the sports media narratives about the supposed "Kardashian Curse" are largely unwarranted, at least in the realm of the NBA.
